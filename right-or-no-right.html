Explanation

What “Constitutional” usually means

Many rights can have reasonable limits (especially time/place/manner rules) as long as the government is not targeting the viewpoint and isn’t effectively banning the right.

1st: speech/press/religion/assembly/petition — allowed limits if neutral & reasonable
4th: searches need probable cause/warrants unless a real exception applies
5th: no forced confession; no second trial for same offense after acquittal
6th: trial rights (counsel, confrontation, speedy/public, jury, charges)
8th: no excessive bail; no cruel/unusual punishment
10th: powers not delegated to federal government are reserved to states/people

Teacher Tip

This game builds OST skill: Judgment → Amendment → Explanation. If students struggle, pause after a scenario and have them identify: (1) government action, (2) right involved, (3) limit on government.
